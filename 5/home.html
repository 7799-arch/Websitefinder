About Lana Del Rey:

Lana Del Rey is an American singer/songwriter known for her cinematic sound, melancholic themes and vintage inspired aesthetic. Emerging in the early 2010s, she quickly gained global attention for her atmospheric music that blends elements of pop, indie, rock and hip hop influences. Her work often explores themes such as love, fame, nostalgia and the darker side of the American dream. About her songs:

Lana Del Rey’s top tracks represent a mix of cinematic anthems and viral resurgences. Summertime Sadness and Young and Beautiful are her biggest hits, the latter written specifically for The Great Gatsby film. Early career staples like Video Games and Born to Die defined her baroque pop sound, while Ultraviolence tracks like West Coast and Brooklyn Baby introduced a grittier, rock influenced style. More recent growth is driven by fan favourites like Cinnamon Girl and her cover of Doin' Time, alongside the formerly unreleased Say Yes to Heaven. Even her brief appearance on Stargirl Interlude has become a massive streaming success, proving the lasting appeal of her melancholic aesthetic across different genres and eras.
