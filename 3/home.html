My Top Four Albums

This list excludes Taylor Swift as she's on another level and it wouldn't be fair to add her.

Stranger in the Alps by Phoebe Bridgers

Written in 2017, this album is one of my favourites, because of its raw, personal feel. The songwriting feels meaningful, such as in songs as Scott Street, with its sad but hopeful feel. This album also has a cover of You Missed My Heart, which I perosnally love, and Phoebe's emotional vocals work well with the melancholy of the song.

Punisher by Phoebe Bridgers

This is Phoebe's sophomore album and although she still keeps to the stripped-back sound of her first album, she builds up with more experimental sounds, with the contrast between folk songs like Graceland Too and the dramatic album-closer of I Know The End. Once again, her songwriting stays the same with its emotional and raw atmosphere, creating a perfect soundscape of fear, sadness, hope and drama.

Beatopia by Beabadoobee

Released in 2022, this is Beatrice Kristi Laus' 2nd studio album. This album, despite fitting comfortably into the indie-rock and pop genre, delves into new areas, different to her previous work. The song, 10:36, experiments with a phone-call and a more electronic sound compared to her last album. One of my personal favourites, Pictures Of Us, was originally written for the 1975, and has a beautiful melancholic, 90s-inspired sound. The album also infuses rock, with songs like Talk and Don't get the deal, this album truly incorporates many genres.

This is How Tommorow Moves by Beabadoobee

This is her most recent album, released in 2024, and it is widely described as her best and most introspective record. It starts with Take a Bite, a pop-rock track, about falling into bad habits and self-destruction. Halfway through we reach Girl Song, an emotional piano-ballad about Laus' life and emotions. It ends with the powerful This Is How It Went , which like the title, describes how a breakup went. Like her prevous work, this record delves into many genres and you can see the development in her songwriting.
