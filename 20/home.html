Tom Welling

Tom Welling is an American actor, director, and former model best known for portraying Clark Kent in the long-running superhero TV series Smallville. Born on April 26, 1977, in Putnam Valley, Welling rose to fame in the early 2000s for his role as the young version of Superman, earning praise for bringing depth and relatability to the iconic character. His performance helped make *Smallville* one of the most popular superhero shows of its time, running for ten seasons. Beyond acting, Welling also worked behind the camera as a director and executive producer on the series. He has since appeared in projects such as Cheaper by the Dozen and the TV series Lucifer, continuing to maintain a strong presence in television and film. Leonardo DiCaprio

Leonardo DiCaprio is one of the most respected and influential actors of his generation. Born on November 11, 1974, in Los Angeles, he first gained worldwide fame for his role in Titanic (1997), which became one of the highest-grossing films of all time. During the 2000s, DiCaprio strengthened his reputation as a serious actor through powerful performances in films such as Catch Me If You Can, The Aviator, The Departed, and Blood Diamond. Known for his dedication to challenging roles and collaborations with director Martin Scorsese, he has received many awards and critical praise throughout his career. In addition to acting, DiCaprio is also well known for his environmental activism through the Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation, which supports projects focused on climate change and wildlife conservation. Paul Walker

Paul Walker was a beloved American actor best known for his starring role as Brian O'Conner in the blockbuster Fast & Furious franchise. Born in 1973 in Glendale, California, he began his career as a child actor in commercials and television shows like Who's the Boss? before breaking into films such as Varsity Blues and Pleasantville. Beyond his Hollywood fame, Walker was a deeply passionate marine biologist at heart and an avid outdoorsman who loved surfing and professional auto racing. He was also a dedicated humanitarian, founding the disaster relief organisation Reach Out Worldwide (ROWW) in 2010 to provide aid to victims of natural disasters. Tragically, Walker passed away in a car accident in 2013 at the age of 40, leaving behind a legacy of kindness and a daughter, Meadow. His final performance in Furious 7 served as a poignant tribute to his life and career, cementing his status as a cinematic icon. In memory of Paul Walker, the song See You Again was created.
