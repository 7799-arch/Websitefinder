Turkey Istanbul

Istanbul is a very vibrant and historic city that connects Europe and Asia across the famous Bosphorus bridge. As the largest city in Turkey, it has been a cultural and political center for thousands of years, once serving as the capital of the Byzantine Empire and later the Ottoman Empire. Beyond its historic sites, Istanbul is also a modern and dynamic city. Its streets are packed with cafés, restaurants, and markets where visitors can enjoy traditional Turkish foods like baklava and kebab. The city’s mix of ancient culture, modern life, and breathtaking views makes it one of the most fascinating travel destinations in the world. Travel guide

Spain Mallorca

As the largest Balearic island, it features a varied landscape with northern mountains, a central agricultural plain, and a sunny, mild Mediterranean climate ideal for year-round visits. Famous for spectacular beaches like Playa de Formentor and rocky, scenic coves such as Cala Deia, the island is a premier destination for boating and swimming. It has over 300 days of sunshine a year, which attracts a lot of toursits. The Porto Pí lighthouse is the third-oldest in the world. The island's "Drac de Na Coca" myth involves a crocodile that was mistakenly believed to be a dragon. Travel guide

Bulgaria Sofia

Sofia, Bulgaria's capital and largest city, is a youthful, green, and historically rich city located at the foot of Vitosha Mountain. As one of Europe's oldest cities, it offers an eclectic mix of Roman ruins, Ottoman mosques, and Soviet-era architecture alongside modern, leafy boulevards, vibrant nightlife, and a relaxed atmosphere. The name "Sofia" comes from the 4th-century Saint Sofia Church, not a person, and it means "wisdom" in Greek. It is recognized as one of the most affordable capital cities in Europe for tourists. Maldives

The Maldives is a stunning tropical nation in the Indian Ocean, renowned as a luxury destination with roughly 1,200 coral islands, turquoise lagoons, and white-sand beaches. It is the world's flattest country, located southwest of India, offering world-class diving, vibrant coral reefs, and exclusive overwater bungalow resorts. The nation is composed of 26 natural coral atolls that form a chain, spanning roughly 870 km north-to-south. It is the lowest-lying country on earth, with no natural island rising higher than 2.5 meters above sea level. Travel guide
